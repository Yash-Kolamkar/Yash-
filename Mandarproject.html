    • Bittium Tough Mobile 2C

    The first device on the list, from the wonderful country that showed us the brand known as Nokia, comes the Bittium Tough Mobile 2C. The device is a rugged smartphone, and it is as tough on the outside as it is inside, because well, Tough is in the name.In terms of software, the device has two operating systems, with the first one being a hardened version of Android 9 and the second one being the brand’s own Secure OS. To add to that, when you purchase the device, you get a free Yubiko 5 NFC security key included, which will add to the security aspect. Coming back to the software, the two different Operating systems are separated with full data separation. The device also comes with an always-on VPN and Bittium’s own mobile device management. The device comes with an IP67 rating and MIL-STD-810G, which basically means the device is really strong and won’t break easily.An additional feature is a hardware-privacy mode, which disables Bluetooth, camera, and the microphone and limits all these functionalities. The pricing for the device is around $1800 (Rs. 1,32,000 approx), considering the security aspect though, it makes a lot of sense to buy this device, regardless of the high price.

    K-iPhone

    If an iPhone just wont be able to do a job for you in terms of keeping your data safe, consider purchasing the K-iPhone. A company that goes by the name of KryptAll has taken a regular iPhone and taken it to the next level in terms of safety and security of your data. The device has it’s firmware changed and there is the addition of its own VoIP applications (by Kryptall) to ensure that all phone calls are fully encrypted. Even KryptAll and law enforcement agencies can’t decrypt your conversations. While this device is good, it will cost you a pretty penny, considering it is priced at $4500 (Rs. 3,30,000 approx), much higher than even something like the recently launched iPhone 12 Pro Max.

    Solarin From Sirin Labs

    The third device on the list is also one of the most expensive devices in the world. Made by Israeli startup Sirin Labs, the Solarin offers the most advanced privacy technology, available outside the agency world (According to the brand).The device offers 256-bit AES encryption, which is something that security and defence forces use to secure their communications amongst each other. The device can be activated via a physical security switch present on the back of the device. To add to all this, the device features a 5.5-inch IPS LCD panel with a resolution QHD+, with the Snapdragon 810 chip and a 23.8-megapixel rear sensor to take images. The price for the same is an exorbitant $13,500 (Rs. 9,90,000 approx).

    Blackphone 2

    The Blackphone 2 was launched by Silent Circle, which is well known due to the Silent OS, an operating system made by the brand. The device is based on Android like other devices on this list, but, it comes with enhanced features that can help address security issues and privacy concerns that might exist with regular smartphones. The Operating system offers an Enterprise Spaces feature, which creates multiple and separate virtual devices on one single device. The company also states that the device features the world’s fastest vulnerability management feature, which can help raise critical and dangerous vulnerabilities within 72 hours of detection or reporting the same. To add to that, the updates and patches come from the brand itself, which means it has total control over the software of the device. In terms of the important specifications, the device comes with a 5.5-inch FHD+ display with Gorilla Glass protection, and, the device is powered by an octa-core Qualcomm Snapdragon processor and features a 13-megapixel sensor from BSI. The device also features a simple design that might fool someone into thinking the device is a regular smartphone, which is something that is frankly better than a device like the Solarin. The device can be bought from Silent Circle’s official site for around $800 (Rs. 59,000 approx), which is not quite high considering what the device can do.

    BlackBerry DTEK50

    The final device on the list, the device comes from the well known company, Blackberry, which has been making devices like this (ex. Boeing Black). The device at the time of it’s launch was known as the world’s most secure Android smartphone. It comes with features like periodic application tracking, which can automatically keep track and monitor the OS and applications present on the smartphone. The feature also alerts the user about the privacy risks that might occur and suggest methods to fix the same. Additionally, there is the inclusion of the Password keeper feature, which enables the user to store all the important passwords at one place, which is also encrypted and protected by another password. In terms of the specifications, the device comes with a 5.2-inch FHD+ scratch-resistant display with a resolution of FHD+. It is powered by the Qualcomm Snapdragon 617 processor and comes with a 13-megapixel autofocus camera towards the back. The device in the current market can be availed for anywhere around $200-400(Rs. 15,000 – Rs. 30,000 approx), which is frankly a steal considering its primary purpose of existence and all that it offers to do the same.
